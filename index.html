Task Breakdown (Plain English)

Click on any task to see a simple explanation of what it means for our team.

1. Data Ingestion & Storage +
What this means: We set up systems that collect raw data — like live GPS locations from buses and timetable schedules — and store them securely in databases. Think of it as building the plumbing that brings water into the house. Nothing else can work until this is in place.
2. Stream & Batch Processing +
What this means: Handling data in two ways. "Stream" processing deals with live data the moment it arrives — updating a map in real time. "Batch" processing analyzes large amounts of old data at once, like running a nightly report to find recurring traffic patterns.
3. AI/ML ETA Models +
What this means: Using algorithms to predict exactly when a bus will arrive at a stop. The model learns from historical patterns — traffic, time of day, weather — to give commuters a reliable Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA). This is our most important deliverable.
4. Prediction API & Analytics +
What this means: Building a digital interface (an API) that other groups — like the team building the mobile app — can call to get ETA predictions from us. When they ask "when is Bus 47 arriving?", our system answers in under a second.
5. Data Validation & Quality +
What this means: Automatic checks that catch corrupt or impossible data before it breaks our models. If a GPS tracker reports a bus in the ocean, or a timestamp jumps backwards in time, these checks flag it so we never train our AI on bad data.
6. Cross-Group Coordination +
What this means: Talking with other sub-groups to agree on contracts. We need to know: what format will the hardware/GPS team send us data in, and what format does the frontend team expect us to return predictions in? This prevents integration failures later.

Opening & Welcome ~2 min+

Say this to start:

"Hey everyone, welcome to our first G2 meeting. My name is [your name] and I am leading this group. Today we are not here to solve everything — we are here to get on the same page, understand what our group is responsible for, and make a simple plan together. This will take about 30 minutes."

"Before we start, let me quickly tell you what Group 2 actually does in this project. We are the data and intelligence team. Think of the whole system like a human body — other groups build the hands, the face, the voice. We are the brain. We collect information, process it, and predict things that the rest of the system needs to work."

What is Our Job? (The 5 Tasks) ~6 min+

Walk through each task in simple words. Point to the map above.

1. Data Ingestion & Storage — "First, we need to collect raw data. For example, where is every bus right now? What time should it arrive? We pull all this data in and store it safely. Nothing else works if this is broken. This is our foundation."

2. Stream & Batch Processing — "Once data comes in, we process it. Some of it we process immediately — like a live GPS update. Some we process later in large amounts — like analysing last month's traffic patterns. Both are important."

3. AI / ML ETA Models — "This is our most exciting task. We build a model that predicts when a bus will arrive. It learns from patterns — traffic, time of day, weather. The better our model, the more useful our whole system is."

4. Prediction API — "Once our model works, we wrap it in an API. Think of an API as a door — other groups knock on it and ask 'when is Bus 47 arriving?' and we answer instantly. The frontend team depends on this."

5. Data Validation — "We also need automatic checks. If a GPS says a bus is in the ocean, that's bad data. We catch and filter these problems before they break our models."

Our Development Plan (4 Phases) ~5 min+

Keep it simple. You are not committing to exact dates yet — just explaining the logical order.

"We will build our work in four steps, one on top of the other:"

  • Phase 1 — Ingestion Pipelines: Set up the system to receive and store data. No data = no work possible.
  • Phase 2 — Processing & Quality Checks: Clean the data, filter bad values, run it through our pipeline reliably.
  • Phase 3 — Model Training: Use the clean data to train our prediction model. Test it. Improve it.
  • Phase 4 — API Serving: Publish the model as an API so other groups can call it from their apps.

"We do not start Phase 3 until Phase 1 and 2 are solid. This order matters."

Talking with Other Groups ~5 min+

This is critical. Other groups depend on us and we depend on them. Be clear about this.

"We cannot work in isolation. There are two conversations we need to have with other groups very soon:"

  • With the hardware / GPS team: We need to know exactly what format they will send us data in. What fields? How often? Over what connection? If we do not agree on this early, our pipeline will break when we connect.
  • With the frontend / app team: They will call our prediction API. We need to agree on what our response looks like — what fields, what units, what speed. They cannot build their app until they know what to expect from us.

"I will take responsibility for setting up these conversations. If anyone already has contacts in those groups, please tell me after this meeting."

Assigning Roles ~6 min+

Ask, don't assign. See who is interested in what first.

"Now I want to hear from you. We have five areas of work. I do not want to just tell everyone what to do — I want the right person on the right task."

"Let me ask — does anyone have experience with databases or data pipelines? Anyone who has done machine learning before? Anyone comfortable building APIs?"

Listen. Take notes in the Minutes section below. Then suggest pairings.

"Here is what I suggest: we work in pairs or small groups per task, so no one is stuck alone. We will confirm assignments today and write them down."

Innovation Ideas (Optional) ~3 min+

Keep this light — just plant the seed. Do not over-discuss today.

"Beyond our core tasks, there are some optional ideas we can explore if time allows during the project:"

  • Weather integration — adjust ETA predictions based on rain or extreme weather.
  • Live dashboards — a visual screen showing real-time bus data for operators.
  • Anomaly detection — automatically flag when something unusual happens, like a bus that is stuck for too long.

"These are not required — but they could make our work stand out. We will revisit once Phase 1 is done."
