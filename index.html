Task Breakdown (Plain English)

Click on any task to see a simple explanation of what it means for our team.

1. Data Ingestion & Storage +
What this means: We set up systems that collect raw data — like live GPS locations from buses and timetable schedules — and store them securely in databases. Think of it as building the plumbing that brings water into the house. Nothing else can work until this is in place.
2. Stream & Batch Processing +
What this means: Handling data in two ways. "Stream" processing deals with live data the moment it arrives — updating a map in real time. "Batch" processing analyzes large amounts of old data at once, like running a nightly report to find recurring traffic patterns.
3. AI/ML ETA Models +
What this means: Using algorithms to predict exactly when a bus will arrive at a stop. The model learns from historical patterns — traffic, time of day, weather — to give commuters a reliable Estimated Time of Arrival (ETA). This is our most important deliverable.
4. Prediction API & Analytics +
What this means: Building a digital interface (an API) that other groups — like the team building the mobile app — can call to get ETA predictions from us. When they ask "when is Bus 47 arriving?", our system answers in under a second.
5. Data Validation & Quality +
What this means: Automatic checks that catch corrupt or impossible data before it breaks our models. If a GPS tracker reports a bus in the ocean, or a timestamp jumps backwards in time, these checks flag it so we never train our AI on bad data.
6. Cross-Group Coordination +
What this means: Talking with other sub-groups to agree on contracts. We need to know: what format will the hardware/GPS team send us data in, and what format does the frontend team expect us to return predictions in? This prevents integration failures later.

Do We Need to Collect Data and Train a Model? ~4 min+

A lot of non-technical members will wonder this. Answer it clearly and simply.

"Yes — and let me explain why both steps matter in plain English."

Collecting data: "Imagine trying to predict the weather without ever having looked outside. You can't. To predict when a bus will arrive, we first need to observe — collect months of historical bus movements, delays, route timings, and traffic conditions. That data is our raw material."

Training a model: "Once we have data, we feed it to a machine learning algorithm. The algorithm looks for patterns — 'Bus 12 is always 4 minutes late on rainy Monday mornings.' It learns these patterns so that next time it rains on a Monday, it automatically adjusts its prediction. We do not hard-code rules — the model figures them out itself from data."

Then what? "Once trained and tested, we freeze the model and put it behind an API. Every time someone asks for an ETA, the model runs in milliseconds and returns its best prediction. It can be retrained later as we collect more data."

Pause. Ask: 'Does that make sense to everyone? Any questions on this?'

What Makes Our Work Stand Out ~3 min+

This is motivating. Say it with energy. Make the team feel proud of their role.

"Every other group depends on us. The mobile app, the display screens, the operator dashboard — none of them can show accurate information without our predictions. We are the engine under the hood."

"What makes a great G2 team stand out is not just making predictions — it is making reliable, fast, and explainable predictions. Here is what we can do to go beyond the basics:"

  • Use real-time weather data — adjust our ETA predictions dynamically when it starts raining or there is an event in the city.
  • Build a live monitoring dashboard — a screen that shows the health of our pipeline and model accuracy in real time. No other group will have this.
  • Add anomaly detection — automatically alert operators when something unusual is happening, like a bus that has been stationary for too long.
  • Track model performance over time — use tools like MLflow to log every experiment and prove our model is improving. This shows professionalism and rigour.

"None of these are required for a pass. But together, they turn a good project into an impressive one."

Our Development Plan (4 Phases) ~4 min+

Keep it simple. You are not committing to exact dates yet — just the logical order.

"We will build our work in four steps, each one depending on the last:"

  • Phase 1 — Ingestion Pipelines: Set up the system to receive and store data. No data = no work possible.
  • Phase 2 — Processing & Quality Checks: Clean the data, filter bad values, make it reliable for training.
  • Phase 3 — Model Training: Use the clean data to train our ETA prediction model. Test it. Improve it. Log results.
  • Phase 4 — API Serving: Wrap the model in a FastAPI service so other groups can call it from their apps.

"Phase 3 cannot start until Phases 1 and 2 are solid. This is not optional — garbage data produces garbage predictions."

Talking with Other Groups ~4 min+

This is critical. Be direct — delays here will block the whole project.

"We cannot work in isolation. Two urgent conversations need to happen this week:"

  • With the hardware / GPS team: What format will they send us data in? What fields? How often — every second, every 10 seconds? Over what protocol? We need a written agreement on this before we build our pipeline.
  • With the frontend / app team: They will call our API. We need to agree upfront — what does our response look like? What fields, what units, how fast do we respond? They cannot build their UI until we confirm this.

"I will set up these meetings. If anyone here already has contacts in those groups, please let me know right after this."

Assigning Roles ~4 min+

Ask, don't just assign. Use the Team Ideas section below to collect interest live.

"Now I want to hear from all of you. We have five task areas. I do not want to just hand out jobs — I want the right person on the right task."

"Let me ask — who has worked with databases before? Who has done any machine learning, even in a course? Who is comfortable with Python? Who wants to learn something completely new?"

Closing & Next Steps ~2 min+

End with clarity. Everyone should leave knowing exactly what they are doing next.

"We covered a lot today. Here is what we are walking away with:"

  • Everyone understands what G2 does and why we matter to the project.
  • We know the tools we will use for each task.
  • We understand why data collection and model training are both necessary.
  • We have a 4-phase plan and role assignments written down.
  • We know which other groups to contact first.
